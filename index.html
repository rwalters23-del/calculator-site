📚 Guide to GLP-1 Medications

GLP-1 medications are FDA-approved drugs that help with weight loss by suppressing appetite and improving blood sugar control.

Weight loss expectations

  • Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy): 15-20% body weight in 12 months
  • Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound): 20-25% body weight in 12 months
  • Average monthly loss: 3-5 lbs/month after titration

Tips for saving

  • Check if insurance covers your medication
  • Consider compounded versions (70% cheaper)
  • Look for manufacturer discount cards
  • Use prescription savings cards
  • Try mail-order for discounts

Q: Are GLP-1 medications safe?

A: Yes, when prescribed by a doctor. Common side effects include nausea, diarrhea, and constipation.

Q: How long do I need to take them?

A: Most people take GLP-1 medications for 6-12 months to achieve their weight loss goals.

Q: What's the difference between Ozempic and Wegovy?

A: Same active ingredient (semaglutide). Wegovy is specifically approved for weight loss, Ozempic is for diabetes but commonly prescribed off-label.

📚 Understanding Breakeven

Your breakeven point is when total revenue equals total costs. Beyond that point, you start making profit.

  • Fixed costs - Rent, salaries, subscriptions, software
  • Variable costs - Materials, shipping, payment fees per unit
  • Contribution margin - Price minus variable cost per unit

Breakeven formula

Breakeven Units = Fixed Costs ÷ (Price - Variable Costs)

How to lower breakeven

  • Reduce fixed costs
  • Increase prices (if possible)
  • Lower variable costs
  • Improve unit economics

How to Use This Calculator

Using our free BMI calculator is simple. Enter your weight in pounds and height in inches. For more accurate results, add your waist measurement, which helps assess腹部 fat distribution—a better predictor of health risks than BMI alone.

Select your gender and age, then click "Calculate BMI" to get your results instantly.

Example Calculation

Let's say you weigh 170 pounds and are 70 inches tall (5'10"):

  • BMI Formula: (170 ÷ 70²) × 703 = 24.5
  • Result: Normal weight (BMI 18.5-24.9)
  • If your waist is 32 inches, your waist-to-height ratio is 0.46 (healthy range)

Why BMI Matters

BMI is a widely-used screening tool that helps identify weight categories that may lead to health problems. While it doesn't directly measure body fat, a high BMI can indicate increased risks for:

  • Heart disease and stroke
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Certain cancers

However, BMI has limitations—it doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat. Athletes with high muscle mass may have a high BMI but be perfectly healthy.

FAQ

How do I calculate BMI manually?

The formula is: BMI = (weight in pounds ÷ height in inches²) × 703. For example, a 170-pound person who is 70 inches tall would calculate: (170 ÷ 4900) × 703 = 24.5.

What is a healthy BMI range?

A BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered normal or healthy. Below 18.5 indicates underweight, 25-29.9 is overweight, and 30+ indicates obesity.

Is BMI accurate for everyone?

BMI is a general screening tool and doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, age, or sex differences. Athletes, elderly individuals, and muscular builds may have inaccurate BMI readings.

Does waist size affect BMI accuracy?

Yes. Waist circumference is an important补充 indicator. Men with waists over 40 inches and women over 35 inches have higher health risks, regardless of BMI.

Understanding your daily calorie needs is essential for:

  • Weight loss - Create a calorie deficit to lose weight sustainably
  • Weight gain - Surplus calories to build muscle
  • Maintenance - Know exactly what to eat to stay the same
  • Athletic performance - Fuel your body properly for activities

FAQ

How many calories do I need to lose weight?

A safe weight loss is 1-2 pounds per week, requiring a 500-1,000 calorie daily deficit. Never go below 1,200 calories/day for women or 1,500 for men without medical supervision.

What is BMR vs TDEE?

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is calories burned at complete rest. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) includes your activity level. Your daily calorie target should be based on TDEE.

How accurate is this calorie calculator?

Our calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, considered the most accurate for estimating calorie needs. However, individual metabolism varies by ~15%, so monitor results and adjust as needed.

Should I eat different macros for weight loss?

Protein is most important for weight loss (0.8-1g per pound of body weight). Beyond that, choose the macro split you can sustain. Low-carb works for some, while others thrive on higher carb diets.

FAQ

How is mortgage interest calculated?

Mortgage interest is calculated monthly on your remaining loan balance. In the early years, most of your payment goes to interest. By the end of the loan, most goes to principal.

What is a good interest rate in 2026?

As of 2026, a competitive 30-year fixed mortgage rate ranges from 6.0% to 7.0% for well-qualified buyers. Rates vary by credit score, down payment, and lender.

Should I choose a 15-year or 30-year mortgage?

A 15-year mortgage saves tens of thousands in interest but requires higher monthly payments. Choose 15-year if you can afford the payments and want to pay off faster. Choose 30-year for lower payments and flexibility.

What is PMI and when do I need it?

PMI is Private Mortgage Insurance required when your down payment is less than 20%. It protects the lender, not you. Once you reach 20% equity, you can request it be removed.

How to Use This Calculator

Our free percentage calculator handles two common tasks: finding what percentage one number is of another, and calculating sale prices after discounts. Both are essential for everyday math.

For percentages: Enter the part (numerator) and the whole (denominator). For discounts: Enter the original price and discount percentage.

Example Calculations

What is 25% of 100?

  • 25 ÷ 100 × 100 = 25
  • Answer: 25

$100 item with 20% off?

  • $100 × (1 - 0.20) = $80
  • You save: $20

Why Percentages Matter

Percentages are used constantly in daily life:

  • Shopping - Calculate discounts and sale prices
  • Tipping - Figure out appropriate gratuity
  • Finance - Interest rates, APR, investment returns
  • Health - Body fat percentage, nutrition labels
  • Business - Profit margins, growth rates

FAQ

How do I calculate percentage of a number?

Multiply the number by the percentage (as a decimal). For example, 20% of 150 = 150 × 0.20 = 30. Or divide by 100 and multiply: 150 ÷ 100 × 20 = 30.

What is the percentage formula?

Percentage = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100. For example, to find what percent of 50 is 10: (10 ÷ 50) × 100 = 20%.

How do I calculate a discount?

Subtract the discount percentage from 100%, then multiply by the original price. Example: $80 item with 25% off = $80 × 0.75 = $60.

How do I find the original number after a percentage increase?

Divide the new number by (1 + percentage as decimal). For $120 after 20% increase: $120 ÷ 1.20 = $100 original.
