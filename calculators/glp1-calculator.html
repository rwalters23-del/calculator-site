How GLP-1 Medications Work

GLP-1 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1) medications are a class of drugs that help with weight loss by:

  • Appetite suppression - Reduce hunger and cravings
  • Slowed digestion - Feel full longer
  • Blood sugar control - Improve insulin sensitivity
  • Reduced food noise - Decrease obsessive thoughts about food

Clinical trials show 15-22% body weight loss over 72 weeks when combined with diet and exercise.

Weight Loss Timeline Expectations

GLP-1 medications work progressively:

  • Weeks 1-4: Initial adjustment, mild side effects, some appetite reduction
  • Weeks 5-12: Steady weight loss begins, typically 5-10 lbs
  • Weeks 12-24: Continued weight loss, often 10-15% total
  • Months 6-12: Maintenance phase, goal weight achieved for many

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do GLP-1 medications cost without insurance?

Without insurance or discounts, GLP-1 medications cost $1,000-$1,350/month. With manufacturer coupons, you may pay as little as $0-$25/month with commercial insurance.

Does insurance cover GLP-1 for weight loss?

Coverage varies widely. Medicare Part D covers some for obesity. Commercial insurance increasingly covers Wegovy and Zepbound, but prior authorization is often required.

What's the difference between Ozempic and Wegovy?

They contain the same ingredient (semaglutide) but are FDA-approved for different uses. Ozempic is for diabetes, Wegovy is specifically for weight loss and prescribed at higher doses.

Can I get compounded GLP-1?

Yes, but be cautious. Reputable compounding pharmacies can offer significant savings. Avoid "online clinics" with red flags - always verify pharmacy credentials with your state board of pharmacy.

How long do I need to take GLP-1 medication?

GLP-1 medications are typically long-term (1-2+ years) for weight loss maintenance. Most people regain weight if they stop medication without lifestyle changes.
