Print on Demand Basics

Print on Demand (POD) lets you sell custom products without inventory. When a customer orders, the printer makes and ships it. Your profit is the difference between selling price and costs.

Popular POD Platforms

  • Redbubble - Art-focused, 10% base fee + markup
  • Teespring - YouTube integration, platform covers fees
  • Printful - Higher quality, integrates with your store
  • Printify - Multiple suppliers, competitive pricing
  • Merch by Amazon - Amazon-powered, $0 upfront

How to Maximize POD Profits

  • Choose products wisely - T-shirts and stickers have best margins
  • Price strategically - Cover costs and leave profit margin
  • Reduce product costs - Use Printify for multiple supplier options
  • Increase average order value - Offer bundles
  • Scale with more designs - More designs = more sales potential

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can you make with Print on Demand?

Earnings vary widely. Some sellers make a few hundred dollars monthly, while successful ones can earn $1,000-5,000+. It depends on design quality, niche, and marketing.

Is POD profitable in 2026?

Yes, POD remains profitable but is competitive. Success requires quality designs, good SEO, and understanding your target market. It's not a "get rich quick" scheme but can be a solid side income.

What POD products have the best margins?

Stickers and digital downloads have the highest margins (80%+) since they cost almost nothing to produce. T-shirts typically have 20-40% margins after all fees.
