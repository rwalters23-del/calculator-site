Understanding BMR vs TDEE

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the calories your body burns at complete rest - just to keep you alive (breathing, heartbeat, cell production).

TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is your BMR plus all physical activity. This is what you should eat to maintain weight.

Weight Loss: Calorie Deficit Explained

To lose weight, you need a calorie deficit - eating fewer calories than your body burns. Here's how it works:

  • 1 lb of fat = 3,500 calories
  • 500 cal/day deficit = 1 lb/week loss
  • 250 cal/day deficit = 0.5 lb/week loss

Safe weight loss is 1-2 lbs/week. Never go below 1,200 cal/day (women) or 1,500 cal/day (men) without medical supervision.

BMR vs TDEE: What's the Difference?

Your BMR accounts for 60-70% of your TDEE. The rest comes from:

  • Thermic Effect of Food (TEF) - 10% - Energy to digest food
  • Non-Exercise Activity (NEAT) - 15% - Walking, fidgeting, chores
  • Exercise (EAT) - 5% - Deliberate workouts

Frequently Asked Questions

How many calories should I eat to lose weight?

Subtract 250-500 calories from your TDEE. A 500-calorie deficit yields ~1 lb/week loss. Don't go below 1,200 cal/day (women) or 1,500 cal/day (men).

What is a healthy calorie intake?

Adults typically need 1,600-2,400 calories/day depending on age, gender, and activity. This calculator provides personalized targets based on your stats.

Should I eat back exercise calories?

Most trackers overestimate calorie burn. If you exercise, add back 50-75% of what the tracker says to be safe.

The Mifflin-St Jeor equation is considered the most accurate for estimating calorie needs. However, individual metabolism varies by ~15%. Track your weight for 2-3 weeks and adjust if needed.
