How Much House Can I Afford?

A common rule is the 28/36 rule:

  • 28% of gross income should go to housing (mortgage, taxes, insurance)
  • 36% of gross income should go to all debt (housing + car + student loans + credit cards)

Example: If you earn $100,000/year ($8,333/month):

  • Max housing: $8,333 × 0.28 = $2,333/month
  • Max total debt: $8,333 × 0.36 = $3,000/month

Understanding Your Mortgage Payment

Your monthly payment includes:

  • Principal - Paying down the loan balance
  • Interest - The cost of borrowing
  • Property Tax - Your share of local taxes (required)
  • Homeowners Insurance - Required to protect your investment
  • PMI - Required if down payment is less than 20%
  • HOA - If your community has fees

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good mortgage rate in 2026?

As of 2026, competitive 30-year fixed rates range from 6.0% to 7.0% for well-qualified buyers. Your rate depends on credit score, down payment, lender, and loan type.

How much house can I afford with $200k salary?

Using the 28% rule, $200,000/year = $16,667/month gross. Max housing = $4,667/mo. With 6.5% rate and 20% down, you could afford roughly a $650,000-700,000 home.

Should I pay points to lower my rate?

Points (1% of loan = 1 point) can lower your rate by 0.25%. Paying points makes sense if you plan to stay in the home 5+ years. Calculate your break-even point.

What is PMI and when can I remove it?

PMI is Private Mortgage Insurance, required when down payment is under 20%. Once you reach 20% equity (request an appraisal), you can request its removal.

Can I negotiate mortgage rates?

Yes! Rates vary between lenders by 0.25-0.5%. Get quotes from at least 3 lenders. Also negotiate - some lenders will match competitor rates.
